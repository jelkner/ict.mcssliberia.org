Lab 1: Binary (Base 2) Numbers

When you learned to write numbers, you learned them using the decimal numeral system (a.k.a. base-10). As you already know from class, when it comes to computers, it's all just bits. Our computers represent numbers in binary (base-2). Today we will learn about the binary number system and how to do arithmetic in binary.

Activity:

Your instructor (a.k.a. Jeff) will explain how to represent numbers in base-2 (binary). You should take notes and ask questions. After the presentation, you will work together with the people in your row to complete Binary Numbers activity sheet.

If you finish early, take a look at the three Scratch programs below.
