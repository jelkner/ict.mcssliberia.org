  4. When you add a zero to the right of a decimal number, it multiplies its value by 10 (For example, 42 becomes 420). What happens to the value of a binary number when you add a zero on the right? (For example, 111 would become 1110).

  5. Are the binary numbers 11 and 0011 the same number? Why or why not?

  6. Would two bits be enough to assign a unique binary number to each vowel in the English language? Justify your answer.
