Web Page Design Project: Part 1

Overview

Today we will start a project that will apply skills from all of our completed lessons in the Getting Down with HTML tutorial. This will be your end of quarter project and will use all the skills you have aquired this quarter. The goal is to have you continue developing this project throughout the rest of the course, improving it as your web development skills improve.

What Do I Need to Do?

  1. Choose a topic for your project. The best projects are the ones you make for a customer - a club you are in, a sports league you play for, a project in our school, a small business a friend or family member is involved in, etc.

    Note: The importance of choosing a good project, by which I mean one in which you have a strong personal interest and a customer with clear goals, can not be overemphasized. The goal is to have you iterate over this project for the rest of the year, improving it as your skills improve. So choose something that would be engaging and fun for you to work on!

  2. Make a new directory inside your website directory for your project. The name you choose should relate to your project.
  3. Copy min.html to [project]/index.html. This will be the start page for your project.
  4. Make a link from your home page to your project directory.
  5. Make a link from your project start page back to your home page.
  6. Make at least 5 pages about your project.
  7. Make links from your project start page to each of the pages of your project.
  8. To earn an A for the project, the following should be found on at least one of the 6 pages (the index page and the other 5 pages):
    • 25 different HTML element types (spread over the whole project)
    • unordered list
    • ordered list
    • definition list
    • table
    • 4 or more images

If you followed each step correctly, you should see your new web pages by clicking on your name here and following the link to your project start page.
